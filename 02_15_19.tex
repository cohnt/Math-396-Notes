\par\noindent $\vec{F}:\R^{2}\to\R^{2}$ (or perhaps $A^{\ptxt{osso}\R^{2}}\to\R^{2}$) velocity of a fluid (could be time dependent).\n
$\iprod{\vec{F},\vec{T}}$ is the tangential velocity. $\displaystyle\int_{X}\iprod{\vec{F},\vec{T}}\,ds$ is the circulation of $\vec{F}$ along $X$.\n
The average tangential velocity is $\frac{\int_{X}\iprod{\vec{F},\vec{T}}\,ds}{\int_{X}ds}$.\n

\par\noindent Suppose $X$ is a circle of radius $r$. Then the average angular velocity is equal to the average tangential velocity times $1$ radian per $r$ units of distance. So it's equal to the circulation divided by $2\pi{}r^{2}$.\n

\par\noindent From Wednesday, for $\vec{F}=(\alpha,\beta)\leftrightarrow\omega=\alpha{}dx+\beta{}dy$, we have circulation
\[
\int_{X}\alpha\,dx+\beta\,dy=\int_{\ptxt{disc}}\paren{\pd{\beta}{x}-\pd{\alpha}{y}}dx\wedge{}dy
\]

\par\noindent and average angular velocity
\[
\frac{\displaystyle\int_{\ptxt{disk}}\pd{\beta}{x}-\pd{\alpha}{y}}{2\pi{}r^{2}}
\]

\par\noindent As $r$ decreases to $0$, we have $\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}\paren{\pd{\beta}{x}-\pd{\alpha}{y}}$. This is the two-dimensional version of the curl.\n

\par\noindent Now, let's return to $\R^{3}$...\n

\exer{For $\vec{p}\in\R^{3}$, $\vec{v_{1}},\vec{v_{2}}\in\R^{3}$ orthonormal, $M_{r}$ parameterized by $\theta\mapsto\vec{p}+r\cos\theta\vec{v_{1}}+r\sin\theta\vec{v_{2}}$,\n
$\vec{F}=\paren{\begin{array}{ccc}\alpha & \beta & \gamma\end{array}}\tpose$ vector field, $\omega=\alpha{}dx+\beta{}dy+\gamma{}dz$, then the average angular velocity is $\frac{\ptxt{circulation of $\vec{F}$ along $M_{r}$}}{2\pi{}r^{2}}$, and as $r$ decreases to $0$, this tends to\n
\inlineeq{
	\frac{1}{2}d\omega(\vec{p})(\vec{v_{1}},\vec{v_{2}})=\frac{1}{2}\iprod{\curl\vec{F}(\vec{p}),\vec{v_{1}}\times\vec{v_{2}}}=\frac{1}{2}\det\paren{\begin{array}{c:c:c}
	| & | & |\\
	\curl\vec{F}(\vec{p}) & \vec{v_{1}} & \vec{v_{2}}\\
	| & | & |
	\end{array}}
}}

\par\noindent In the general case...\n

\par\noindent For $M$ compact oriented $2$-manifold in $\R^{3}$, the circulation of $\vec{F}$ along $\partial{}M$ is
\[
\int_{\partial{}M}\omega=\int_{M}d\omega=\int_{M}d\omega({\overbrace{\vec{v_{1}},\vec{v_{2}}}^{\mathclap{\substack{\ptxt{positively-oriented basis for $\Tau_{\vec{p}}M$}\\ \\}}}})\,ds=\int_{M}\iprod{\curl\vec{F},\vec{v_{1}}\times\vec{v_{2}}}\,ds=\int_{M}\iprod{\curl\vec{F},\vec{N}}\,ds
\]
